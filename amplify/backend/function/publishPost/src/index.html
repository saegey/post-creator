    TIP RECOGNIZES, REWARDS STUDENTS’ ACHIEVEMENTS

    By
    PUBLISHED: April 26, 1996 at 4:00 a.m. | UPDATED: July 31, 2021 at 2:26 p.m.

    TIP, Duke University’s Talent Identification Program, is honoring the country’s most academically talented students in a series of recognition ceremonies.

    Numerous Seminole County students will be among the 14,000 seventh-graders invited to state or grand recognition ceremonies.

    “Over the years, we have seen many instances of young people whose abilities were underestimated or overlooked by their teachers or their peers . . . and those talents go to waste,” said David Goldstein, TIP’s executive director. “By identifying bright youngsters at an early age and recognizing their abilities, we begin the process of nurturing their talents.”

    To be invited to the Grand Recognition Ceremony, students had to score higher than 70 percent of college-bound high school seniors in the math or verbal section of the Scholastic Aptitude Test.

    Of the 65,400 students who entered the 1995-96 Talent Search, only 1,605 qualified for this ceremony, which is set for June 3 at Duke University in Durham, N.C.
